# Peckish

*A killdeer on the tideline — eat or fade away.*

## The story

You are a killdeer: a small, quick shorebird that sprints, stops dead, and
pecks. The tide is your dinner table — every wave that crashes in leaves a
band of fresh wet sand crawling with worms — but the same surf that feeds
you will happily tumble you head-over-tail. Somewhere up by the dunes your
eggs are waiting to hatch. Keep your belly full, keep your chicks alive,
and run up the score before your metabolism runs you down.

## Controls

| Input | Action |
| --- | --- |
| **D-pad** | Scurry |
| **B (hold)** | Sprint — faster, but burns energy quicker |
| **A** | Peck — eat a surfaced worm; flip a crab, peck it again to finish it |
| **Crank** | Broken-wing display — keep cranking to keep limping; slow, but lures the circling gull onto you |

## How to play

Your energy bar (top left) **never stops draining** — walking costs
energy, sprinting costs more, limping through the broken-wing act and
wading in the shallows cost extra on top. The only way to refill it is to
eat. When the bar hits zero, the run ends.

**Worms** announce themselves as bubbling spots in the sand, then surface
for a short window (they blink just before diving back down). Walk up and
peck. Worms in the dark **wet band** a wave just left are worth more
energy (14 vs 9) and more points (80 vs 50) than dry-sand worms — the
best feeding is always where the danger just was. Every receding wave
also sprouts a rich burst of fresh worm spots right behind it.

**Waves** come in from the right on a warning: "WAVE!" flashes, the warn
sound plays, and the water pulls back a touch before it surges. Get clear!
If you're caught in the surge you're tumbled — spun, swept shoreward, and
docked 15 energy. Chicks caught in the water are **washed away for good**.
Rare **sneaker waves** ("BIG WAVE!") reach about 55 pixels further up the
beach than the foam suggests. Surviving any wave is worth 25 points.

**Eggs** appear in bare scrapes near the dune grass on the left. Stand
over one to brood it: a progress arc fills in about 2 seconds, and a chick
joins your flock (100 points). Wander off and the arc drains; leave an egg
too long (it blinks near the end) and it's gone. You can raise up to
**3 chicks**. They trail behind you, snap up any surfaced worm they walk
over (you get half the energy, full points), and each chick adds **+25%
to every point you score** — a full flock means x1.75 on everything.

## Scoring

| Feat | Points |
| --- | --- |
| Dry-sand worm | 50 |
| Wet-sand worm | 80 |
| Finishing a flipped crab | 150 |
| Luring the gull into an empty grab | 200 |
| Hatching a chick | 100 |
| Surviving a wave | 25 |

All points are multiplied by the flock bonus (+25% per chick). Your best
score is saved on the device.

## Hazards

- **Waves** — telegraphed surge from the right. Tumbles you (-15 energy),
  washes away chicks. Sneaker waves reach much further. The reward for the
  risk: the freshest, richest worms are in the band it leaves behind.
- **Crabs** — scuttle out of the surf and race you to surfaced worms
  (they will steal them). Touching one costs a pinch (-10 energy, brief
  stun). Peck a crab to flip it on its back; peck the flipped crab within
  3 seconds to finish it for 150 points.
- **The gull** — arrives with a screech and circles as a ground shadow
  over its chosen mark. It prefers your chicks (75% of the time, if you
  have any). Crank the broken-wing display while it circles to pull its
  eye onto you instead — then sprint clear as it commits its dive. A hit
  on you costs 16 energy; a snatched chick is gone; an empty grab of sand
  is worth 200 points.
- **The jogger** — a blinking `*!*` at the top or bottom edge marks the
  lane, then footfalls come pounding through the dry sand. Getting
  underfoot costs 12 energy, knocks you flying, and scatters your flock.

## Tips

1. **Live in the wet band.** Wet worms give ~50% more energy and points,
   and every wave replants the band. Treat each "WAVE!" warning as a
   dinner bell — back off, let it recede, then rush the fresh bubbles.
2. **The warning is the pull-back.** When the waterline twitches seaward,
   the surge is one second away. Sneaker waves reach a lot further than
   normal ones — when "BIG WAVE!" flashes, retreat well past where you
   think is safe.
3. **Sprint in bursts, not by habit.** Sprinting adds 150% to your drain
   rate. Use it to beat a crab to a worm or to dodge a dive, then let go
   of B.
4. **Crabs are food too.** A flip-then-finish is 150 points — nearly two
   wet worms — and removes a worm thief. The flip lasts 3 seconds, so
   don't dawdle.
5. **Lure the gull the moment you hear the screech.** Once it's circling
   a chick, a short crank pulls it onto you permanently. Keep moving as
   it commits: dodging the dive is 200 points and everyone stays alive.
6. **Brood early, but on a full stomach.** Chicks are a permanent +25%
   score multiplier and free worm-vacuums that feed you half of what
   they eat — but standing still on an egg costs ~5 energy per hatch, and
   chicks near the surf line are gull bait and wave bait. Herd them left.
